Vehicle Draw Procedure and Information

Reference Guide | By Jacob Duus

Steps for Vehicle Use

Vehicle Pickup:

  1. Complete the vehicle request form.
  2. Retrieve the key from the key rack labeled for you or your department.
  3. Proceed with your trip.

Vehicle Return:

  1. Refill the gas tank before returning.
  2. Return the vehicle to the shop.
  3. Complete the driver's log in the vehicle.
  4. Remove all personal belongings from the vehicle.
  5. Place the key in the drop box.

Important Information and Procedures

Complete the Request Form

The online form is required for all vehicle requests. Verbal requests will not be accepted.

Emergency situations may allow for immediate vehicle assignment if one is available. Lack of planning does not constitute an emergency. Emergencies include: threats to life, limb, or eyesight, fire, or vehicle rescue operations.

Submit Requests as Early as Possible

Early requests allow adequate time for planning, vehicle assignment based on campus-wide needs, and completion of necessary maintenance and repairs.

Request Vehicles for Your Actual Pickup Time

The request form specifies a PICKUP date and time for when you will take possession of the vehicle.

Example: If you request a vehicle for November 10th, the vehicle will be available on November 10th. If you need the vehicle on the evening of November 9th, request it for that date and time.

Return Vehicles on Schedule

Return vehicles by the designated date and time specified in your request. A properly returned vehicle includes: a full fuel tank, removal of all personal belongings, parking by the garage, a completed driver's log, and keys placed in the drop box.

Timely returns ensure smooth scheduling and availability for subsequent users.

Example: A vehicle returning Tuesday evening may be scheduled to depart again early Wednesday morning.

Vehicle Assignment

Vehicles are assigned based on campus-wide availability and requirements, including passenger capacity and cargo needs. Personal preferences cannot be accommodated in the assignment process.

Driver Logs

Complete the driver's log upon return. Document any damages or issues that occurred during your trip—this information is essential for maintenance and repairs. If no issues occurred, note that as well.

Please communicate vehicle concerns directly through the driver's log or to the mechanics. The mechanics understand that issues occur. Simply inform them of what happened.

Items Left in Vehicles

You are responsible for removing all personal belongings from the vehicle. Items left behind will be set aside and placed in the lost and found at the mechanic's convenience. If an item is not important enough to remember to take with you, please understand it is not a priority for staff to track you down.

Special Needs or Concerns

Provide a brief description of your needs, purpose, or concerns in the designated field. Extensive detail is not necessary.

Multiple Consecutive Trips

If you are planning multiple trips within a short timeframe with the same passengers and cargo, request the vehicle for the entire duration.

Example: A basketball team traveling to Aberdeen on Thursday, returning Friday, then departing for Fargo on Saturday and returning Monday should request the vehicle from Thursday through Monday and note the multiple destinations in the request form.
