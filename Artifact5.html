1. Purpose and Scope

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the process for checking out and checking in college fleet vehicles to ensure:

  • Vehicle safety and operational readiness
  • Liability protection for the college and drivers
  • Accurate maintenance tracking and scheduling
  • Proper documentation of vehicle condition and usage

This SOP applies to all college fleet vehicles including:

  • 2 buses
  • 5 twelve-passenger vans
  • 4 vans/crossovers
  • 2 cars

2. Roles and Responsibilities

2.1 Fleet Manager

  • Receives and reviews vehicle requests from drivers
  • Assigns appropriate vehicles based on trip requirements and availability
  • Notifies mechanics of scheduled checkouts
  • Maintains master schedule of vehicle assignments
  • Reviews all completed inspection forms and driver logs
  • Coordinates follow-up on reported issues

2.2 Mechanic

  • Performs pre-trip vehicle inspections (1 hour allocated)
  • Completes Vehicle Checkout Inspection Form
  • Stages vehicles in designated area when approved for use
  • Performs post-trip vehicle inspections upon return
  • Completes Vehicle Check-In Inspection Form
  • Documents and reports all damage, defects, or maintenance needs
  • Removes and catalogs items left in vehicles

2.3 Driver

  • Submits vehicle request to Fleet Manager in advance
  • Picks up keys at scheduled checkout time
  • Conducts brief walk-around inspection before departure
  • Maintains Driver Log Book during trip
  • Returns vehicle to staging area at scheduled time
  • Completes Driver Log Book with any incidents or issues
  • Returns keys to designated drop box
  • Claims any personal items within 24 hours of return

3. Vehicle Checkout Process

3.1 Request and Assignment (Fleet Manager)

  1. Receive vehicle request from authorized driver
  2. Verify driver authorization and valid license
  3. Assign appropriate vehicle based on:
    • Number of passengers
    • Trip duration and distance
    • Vehicle availability
    • Special requirements
  4. Document scheduled checkout date and time
  5. Notify mechanic of upcoming checkout at least 24 hours in advance

3.2 Pre-Trip Inspection (Mechanic)

Time Allocation: 1 hour

  1. Retrieve vehicle and move to inspection bay
  2. Complete Vehicle Checkout Inspection Form (see Section 6)
  3. Document checkout date and time on form
  4. Perform comprehensive inspection using checklist
  5. Document any existing damage with notes and/or photos
  6. Address any maintenance issues:
    • Minor issues (cosmetic, non-safety): Document and proceed
    • Major issues (safety or operational): Report to Fleet Manager immediately; vehicle may not be released
  7. Verify vehicle readiness:
    • Fuel level at 3/4 tank or higher
    • All fluids at proper levels
    • Tire pressures correct
    • All safety equipment present
  8. Place completed Driver Log Book in vehicle
  9. Clean interior if necessary
  10. Move vehicle to staging area
  11. Notify Fleet Manager vehicle is ready
  12. File completed Checkout Inspection Form

3.3 Vehicle Release (Driver)

  1. Arrive at scheduled checkout time
  2. Pick up keys from Fleet Manager or designated location
  3. Conduct brief exterior walk-around inspection
  4. Verify Driver Log Book is in vehicle
  5. Report any immediate concerns to mechanic before departure
  6. Document actual departure time in Driver Log Book
  7. Depart on scheduled trip

4. Vehicle Check-In Process

4.1 Vehicle Return (Driver)

  1. Return vehicle to designated staging area at scheduled time
  2. Document actual return time in Driver Log Book
  3. Complete Driver Log Book sections:
    • Total mileage
    • Fuel level
    • Any incidents, accidents, or mechanical issues
    • Any damage noticed during trip
    • General condition notes
  4. Leave Driver Log Book in vehicle
  5. Remove all personal belongings
  6. Place keys in designated drop box
  7. If immediate mechanical issues exist, notify Fleet Manager or mechanic directly

4.2 Post-Trip Inspection (Mechanic)

Time Allocation: 1 hour

  1. Retrieve vehicle from staging area within 4 hours of return
  2. Review Driver Log Book for reported issues
  3. Document check-in date and time on Vehicle Check-In Inspection Form
  4. Perform comprehensive inspection using checklist (see Section 6)
  5. Compare condition to pre-trip inspection form
  6. Document any new damage, noting:
    • Location and description
    • Severity (minor/major)
    • Whether reported in Driver Log Book
  7. Check for items left in vehicle:
    • Remove all items
    • Log items on Lost and Found form
    • Store in designated lost and found area
    • Items become college property if unclaimed after 30 days
  8. Note fuel level and mileage
  9. Identify maintenance needs:
    • Immediate: Vehicle out of service until repaired
    • Scheduled: Plan for next available maintenance window
    • Monitoring: Document for tracking
  10. Clean vehicle interior and exterior as needed
  11. Return vehicle to fleet parking area
  12. File completed Check-In Inspection Form with Fleet Manager
  13. Notify Fleet Manager of:
    • Any unreported damage
    • Required maintenance or repairs
    • Vehicle availability status

4.3 Documentation Review (Fleet Manager)

  1. Review both checkout and check-in inspection forms
  2. Compare for discrepancies
  3. Review Driver Log Book
  4. Follow up on:
    • Unreported damage
    • Repeated issues with specific vehicles or drivers
    • Maintenance scheduling needs
  5. File all documentation in vehicle history folder
  6. Update vehicle maintenance tracking system

5. Damage and Issue Protocols

5.1 Damage Discovery During Checkout Inspection

  • Minor cosmetic damage: Document with photos/notes, proceed with checkout
  • Safety-related damage: Report to Fleet Manager immediately, do not release vehicle
  • Operational issues: Assess severity; consult Fleet Manager on release decision

5.2 Issues Reported in Driver Log Book

  • Mechanic verifies reported issues during check-in inspection
  • If issue affects safety, vehicle is removed from service
  • Document all findings on check-in form
  • Schedule repairs as appropriate

5.3 Unreported Damage Found During Check-In

  • Document thoroughly with photos and detailed notes
  • Compare to checkout inspection form to confirm damage is new
  • Report to Fleet Manager with recommendation on follow-up
  • Fleet Manager contacts driver for explanation if necessary

5.4 Items Left in Vehicles

  • Mechanic removes all items during check-in inspection
  • Items are logged with:
    • Date found
    • Vehicle number
    • Description of item(s)
    • Driver name from trip
  • Driver notified via email of found items
  • Items held for 30 days in designated lost and found area
  • Unclaimed items after 30 days become college property and may be disposed of or donated

7. Forms and Documentation

7.1 Required Forms

  • Vehicle Checkout Inspection Form (Section 6)
  • Vehicle Check-In Inspection Form (Section 6)
  • Driver Log Book (provided in each vehicle)
  • Lost and Found Log

7.2 Record Retention

  • All inspection forms: Maintained in vehicle history file for 3 years
  • Driver Log Books: Maintained for 3 years
  • Incident reports: Maintained permanently
  • Maintenance records: Maintained for life of vehicle

7.3 Filing System

  • Fleet Manager maintains master files organized by vehicle number
  • Each vehicle has dedicated folder containing:
    • Chronological inspection forms
    • Driver log books
    • Maintenance records
    • Incident reports
    • Registration and insurance documentation

8. Definitions

Checkout Time: The scheduled time when driver picks up vehicle keys and departs
Check-In Time: The actual time when driver returns vehicle to staging area
Staging Area: Designated parking location for vehicles ready for pickup or awaiting inspection
Fleet Manager: Individual responsible for vehicle assignments and scheduling
Driver Log Book: Document maintained in vehicle for driver to record trip details and issues
Lost and Found Period: 30-day window for drivers to claim items left in vehicles

9. Safety and Compliance

9.1 Safety Standards

  • No vehicle shall be released for use if safety equipment is missing or non-functional
  • Any brake, steering, or critical system issues result in immediate out-of-service status
  • All required safety inspections must be current

9.2 Regulatory Compliance

  • All vehicles maintained according to manufacturer recommendations
  • State and federal motor vehicle regulations followed
  • Required inspections and registrations kept current
